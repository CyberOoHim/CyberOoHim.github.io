1. 極淨主義 (POJ: Ke̍k-chēng chú-gī, 英: Minimalism, 日: ミニマリズム)

定義：一款設計風格，強調簡單、機能性，hām 放離 無必要元素 ê 自由。極淨主義 ê 作品，定定用有限 ê 佮色、幾何形體、kap 料身，來 對焦 tī 作品 ê 本質 kap 空間感。

例 1：彼間建築師事務所設計一間新 ê 美術館，in 用極淨主義風格，hō͘ 空間感覺 khah 曠闊、khah 定著。In 用大 phiáⁿ(鉼) 白壁、紅毛塗地板、kap 闊面窗仔，hō͘ 自然光 thang 規个 iā(掖)--入來，hō͘ 展覽品 hiáⁿ-ba̍k(顯目)。這个空間設計，hō͘ 參觀者 ē-tàng chù-bo̍k(注目) tī 藝術品本身，袂 hō͘ 過頭濟 ê chng-thāⁿ(妝娗) kā 注意力 khiú(搝)--去。

例 2：彼个 hoa-sióng(花尚=fashion) 設計師 kō͘(怙) 極淨主義 ê 作品出名。伊 ê 衫，定定用 孤色調、清氣 ê 線逝、kap 高品質 ê 布身，來 hō͘ 人形體 ê 自然媠 thang iáⁿ-ba̍k(影目)。伊 ê 設計理念是「khah 少 就是 khah 濟」，鼓舞人 ùi 複雜 ê 物質世界--ni̍h 走脫，lám-phō(攬抱) 簡單 koh 優雅 ê 生活方式。
---
Definition: A design style that emphasizes simplicity, functionality, and freedom from unnecessary elements. Minimalist works often utilize a limited color palette, geometric shapes, and essential materials to focus on the essence of the work and create a sense of spaciousness.

Ex 1: An architectural firm designed a new art museum using a minimalist approach, creating a spacious and serene atmosphere. They incorporated large white walls, concrete floors, and expansive windows to allow natural light to flood the space, highlighting the artwork on display. This spatial design allowed visitors to focus on the art itself without being distracted by excessive ornamentation.

Ex 2: The fashion designer is known for her minimalist creations. Her clothing often features monochromatic colors, clean lines, and high-quality fabrics to accentuate the natural beauty of the human form. Her design philosophy of "less is more" encourages people to break free from the complexities of the material world and embrace a simple and elegant lifestyle.

2. 新藝術 (POJ: Sin-gē-su̍t, 英: Art Nouveau, 日: アール・ヌーヴォー)

定義：一款 19 世紀尾到 20 世紀頭 tī 歐美 chhia-iāⁿ(奢颺) ê 藝術 kap 設計運動，特點是強調受著自然開破--來 ê 有機形態，親像植物、花、kap 動物 ê 流動線逝，定定有彎斡 ê 曲線、無對稱 ê 構圖、kap chng-thāⁿ-sèng(妝娗性) ê 詳細。

例 1：Tī Pá-lih(Paris)，你看會著濟濟新藝術風格 ê 厝宅，in ê 外觀，定定有複雜 ê 鐵仔工藝、鬥彩玻璃窗、hām ùi 自然揣著靈感 ê 花草來妝娗，hō͘ 人感覺優雅 iū-koh 浪漫。

例 2：Chhè-koh(Czech) 畫家 Mú-hah(Mucha) ê 作品，是新藝術風格 ê 代表作。伊 ê 海報，常常描畫媠姿娘，in 頭鬃長長若 liû-súi(流水)，身穿若捲螺 ê 長紗裙，四圍用花 kap 藤來妝娗，有若眠夢也神秘 ê 氛圍。

例 3：新藝術風格 mā 影響著家具、珠寶、kap 其他妝娗藝術 ê 設計。新藝術風格 ê 家具，捷捷有曲線、木雕、kap 鬥彩玻璃來妝娗，hō͘ 人感覺幼路而且華麗。
---
Definition: An art and design movement that flourished in Europe and America from the late 19th to early 20th centuries, characterized by its emphasis on organic forms inspired by nature, such as the flowing lines of plants, flowers, and animals, often incorporating sinuous curves, asymmetrical compositions, and decorative details.

Ex 1: In Paris, you can find many buildings in the Art Nouveau style, their facades often adorned with 
 ironwork, stained glass windows, and nature-inspired motifs, evoking a sense of elegance and romance.

Ex 2: The works of Czech painter Alphonse Mucha are iconic examples of Art Nouveau. His posters often depict beautiful women with long flowing hair, dressed in swirling gowns, surrounded by floral and vine-like decorations, creating a dreamlike and mystical atmosphere.

Ex 3: Art Nouveau also influenced the design of furniture, jewelry, and other decorative arts. Art Nouveau furniture often features curved lines, carved wood, and stained glass, conveying a sense of refinement and opulence.

3. 現代主義 (POJ: Hiān-tāi chú-gī, 英: Modernism, 日: モダニズム)

定義：20 世紀頭出現 ê 藝術 kap 文化運動，強調講無愛傳統 ê 形式 kap 價值觀，欲來攬抱新科技、社會變遷、kap 藝術表現 ê 款式。現代主義定定會注重機能、簡潔、kap 抽象美感，反對過頭 ê 妝娗 kap 傳統 ê 審美標準。

例 1：現代主義家具 tī 20 世紀有深闊 ê 影響，kā 人對家具 ê 想法透底轉變--去。現代主義家具，親像 Lō-khó͘-bu-jī-è(Le Corbusier) 設計 ê LC4 the(䖙)椅，iah-sī Ma-sé Bu-ló͘-ià(Marcel Breuer) 設計 ê Sè-su̍h-khah(Cesca) 椅，常常用鋼管、皮革、be-lí-a板遮个新材料，kap 簡單 ê 幾何形體，來表現現代主義 ê 簡單、機能、kap 優雅。遮个家具，m̄-nā 四序 koh 實用，mā 挑戰傳統家具彼號厚枝葉 ê 風格，成做 20 世紀設計 ê 經典作品，到今 iu-goân 真時行。

例 2：米國抽象表現主義畫家 Chiah-sng Phó͘-lak(Jackson Pollock) ê 作品，是現代主義繪畫 ê 代表。伊 kā 色料滴 tī 畫布頂懸，無用 bín-á(抿仔)，來表現自由、自然而然、kap 潛意識 ê 創作過程。伊 ê 作品挑戰傳統 ê 畫圖技術 kap 美學觀，kā 藝術表現 ê 可能性 hùn-khoah(楦闊)--出去。
---
Definition: An artistic and cultural movement that emerged in the early 20th century, emphasizing a rejection of traditional forms and values in favor of embracing new technologies, social changes, and modes of artistic expression. Modernism often focuses on functionality, simplicity, and abstract aesthetics, rejecting excessive ornamentation and traditional aesthetic standards.

Ex 1: Modernist furniture had a profound impact on the 20th century, revolutionizing the way people thought about furniture. Modernist pieces, such as the LC4 Chaise Longue by Le Corbusier or the Cesca Chair by Marcel Breuer, often utilized new materials like tubular steel, leather, and plywood, along with simple geometric forms, to embody modernism's principles of simplicity, functionality, and elegance. These pieces were not only comfortable and practical but also challenged the ornate styles of traditional furniture, becoming iconic designs of the 20th century that remain popular to this day.

Ex 2: The works of American abstract expressionist painter Jackson Pollock are iconic examples of modernist painting. He dripped paint onto canvases without using brushes, expressing a free, spontaneous, and subconscious creative process. His works challenged traditional painting techniques and aesthetics, expanding the possibilities of artistic expression.

4. Gu-lá-hūi-thì、皂壁 (POJ: Gu-lá-hūi-thì、chō-piah, 英: Graffiti, 日: グラフィティ)

定義：Tī 公共空間 he̍k-chiá 私人財產頂頭，無人允準，就用噴漆、mà-khoh 筆、iah-sī 其他材料，畫 無就是 寫 ê 圖像 he̍k-chiá 文字，不時傳達視覺衝擊、社會評論、iah-sī 個人表達。

例 2：一位廢棄 ê 工廠 ê 壁頂，Gu-lá-hūi-thì 藝術家 kā 幾若幅大型、色水鮮沢 ê 抽象壁畫噴--起去。遮个 Gu-lá-hūi-thì，無特定 ê 意思 iah-sī 訊息，煞 kā  活力 kap 想像力，注--入去已經無性命 ê 空間，ho͘ 成做一个戶外 ê 藝術畫廊，hō͘ 過路人 thang 欣賞。

例 3：Tī 一堵捷運站邊仔 ê 壁頂，濟款 Gu-lá-hūi-thì 重重疊疊，成做一齣多彩 ê 視覺辦桌。有一寡 Gu-lá-hūi-thì ，是用 pho(泡)仔字體 寫 ê 名，若親像簽名，leh 標記 in ê 存在。另外有一寡，畫了 khah 厚工，有 khāh-thún(cartoon) 人物、抽象花草、koh 有政治標語，反映都市內底，多元文化 kap 意識型態 ê 相挵 kap 融合。
---
Definition: Images or lettering painted, drawn, or scribbled on a public space or private property without permission, often using spray paint, markers, or other materials, frequently conveying visual impact, social commentary, or personal expression.

Ex 1: In the back alleys of cities, graffiti artist Banksy's works showcase social critique and dark humor. His graffiti often depicts images of political figures, social issues, and pop culture, using satire to challenge our perceptions of modern society. His works, from London to New York, have been both praised and controversial, as some consider graffiti to be art while others view it as vandalism.

Ex 2: On the walls of an abandoned factory, graffiti artists spray-painted large, colorful abstract murals. These graffiti, without specific meanings or messages, injected vibrancy and imagination into the otherwise lifeless space, transforming it into an outdoor art gallery for passersby to enjoy.

Ex 3: On a wall near a subway station, various graffiti overlap, creating a colorful visual feast. Some graffiti are names written in bubble letters, like signatures, marking their presence. Others are more elaborate, featuring cartoon characters, abstract patterns, and political slogans, reflecting the clash and fusion of diverse cultures and ideologies within the city.

5. Páu-hāu-suh (POJ: Páu-hāu-suh, 英: Bauhaus, 日: バウハウス)

定義：一款 1919 年到 1933 年 tī 德國 heng(興)--起來 ê 藝術、設計、kap 建築學校 hām 運動，強調 kā 藝術 kap 工藝鬥搭--起來，koh-ū kā 設計簡化、機能化。Páu-hāu-suh ê 理念深濶影響著 20 世紀 ê 現代設計，in 強調「型綴機能」ê 原則，認為講物件 ê 設計應該愛 kō͘(怙) 按算 ê 功能做基底，m̄-sī 去過頭 tùi-tiōng(對重) 裝娗。

例 1：Páu-hāu-suh 設計師捷捷用幾何形體、原色、kap 新奇 ê 材料，親像鋼管、玻璃、kap 紅毛塗，來創作實用 koh 媠氣 ê 家具、建築、hām 日常用品。譬論講，Páu-hāu-suh 設計師 Ma-sé Bu-ló͘-ià(Marcel Breuer) 設計 ê Óa-si̍h-lì(Wassily) 椅子，就用彎鋼管來做椅仔腳 kap 椅hōaⁿ(扞)，用皮革來做椅面，簡單、勇在，koh 足四序，成做一件現代家具設計 ê 經典作品。

例 2：Páu-hāu-suh ê 影響，你 tī 現代 ê 家具、平面設計、kap 建築--ni̍h 看會著。濟濟現代 ê 椅仔、桌仔、kap 電火，lóng 展現 Páu-hāu-suh 風格 ê 特色，in 線逝清氣、機能性強，koh ùi-tiōng(對重) 材料 ê 使用。比論講，Apple 公司 ê 產品，親像 iPhone、iPad、kap MacBook，lóng 有 Páu-hāu-suh 風格 ê 影響，in 設計極淨、致心用者體驗，而且用--ê 是高品質 ê 材料。
---
Definition: An art, design, and architecture school and movement that emerged in Germany from 1919 to 1933, emphasizing the integration of art and craft and promoting simplified, functional design. Bauhaus principles had a profound impact on 20th-century modern design, advocating for "form follows function," where the design of an object should be based primarily on its intended purpose rather than excessive ornamentation.

Ex 1: Bauhaus designers often used geometric shapes, primary colors, and innovative materials like tubular steel, glass, and concrete to create functional and aesthetically pleasing furniture, buildings, and everyday objects. For example, the Wassily Chair, designed by Bauhaus designer Marcel Breuer, utilizes bent tubular steel for the frame and leather for the seat, creating a simple, sturdy, and comfortable chair that has become a classic piece of modern furniture design.

Ex 2: The influence of Bauhaus can be seen in modern furniture, graphic design, and architecture. Many contemporary chairs, tables, and lamps exhibit characteristics of the Bauhaus style, featuring clean lines, strong functionality, and an emphasis on material use. For instance, Apple products like the iPhone, iPad, and MacBook all show the influence of Bauhaus in their minimalist design, focus on user experience, and use of high-quality materials.

6.  Sái-bó͘-phàng-khoh (POJ: Sái-bó͘-phàng-khoh, 英: Cyberpunk, 日: サイバーパンク)

定義：一款科幻類型，定定設定 tī 未來城市環境，特徵有前沿科技，敗害 ê 社會 kap 極大貧富崁差，主題牽磕著人機相佮、人工智能、hām 人類機能擴張遮个元素。Sái-bó͘-phàng-khoh(Cyberpunk) ê 作品，捷捷探討科技對人類社會、身份認同、kap 道德 ê 影響，而且不時有 反Iu-thó͘-phi-ah(Utopia) ê 氣氛。

例 1：電影《Blade Runner》是 Sái-bó͘-phàng-khoh 類型 ê 一个經典例。故事設定 tī 未來 ê 洛杉磯，科技前沿，m̄-koh 為著環境汙染嚴重，社會無平等，道德敗害 leh 受苦，電影探討一寡主題，親像人工智能、人類身份認同，kap 科技進步 ê 後果。這齣電影對複製人 ê 探討，致起人類本質是啥、AI 敢可能有知覺，koh-ū 人類 kap 機器之間霧霧 ê 界線，án-ne ê 問題。電影 ê 視覺風格，包括烏暗 ê 都市景觀、閃爍 ê ní-àng(neon)燈，kap 雨沃 kah 澹kô͘-ko͘ ê 氣氛，愈幫贊 Sái-bó͘-phàng-khoh ê 標誌性美學。

例 2：日本 a-ní-me(anime) 電影《Akira》mā 是 Sái-bó͘-phàng-khoh  ê 代表作。故事設定 tī 重建了後科技進步 ê 東京，m̄-koh iû-goân chham 社會無在 leh 捙拚，電影探討少年悖逆、幫派暴力，kap 政府陰謀。伊 ê 視覺風格，包括 ní-àng(neon)燈、懸懸 ê thàng(迵)天大樓，kap 速度感，hō͘ 人印象不止仔深。《Akira》內底，超能力 ê 議題，引人思考人類潛力 ê 界線，koh-ū 失站節 ê 科技進步，會有啥物無法度料算 ê 後果。電影內底，東京都 ê 滅無 kap 重建，象徵科技進步 kap 社會 tōng-hàiⁿ(盪幌) ê 關係，hō͘ 人看著 Sái-bó͘-phàng-khoh ê 世界觀。

例 3：Sái-bó͘-phàng-khoh 風格 mā 影響著時裝、音樂、kap 遊戲設計。Sái-bó͘-phàng-khoh 時裝，捷捷用皮革、金屬、kap 螢光色，來表現未來感 kap 悖逆 ê 精神。Sái-bó͘-phàng-khoh ê 音樂，不時用電子音樂、合成器、kap 工業噪音，來成造烏暗、神秘 ê 氣氛。Sái-bó͘-phàng-khoh 遊戲，定定 hō͘ 玩家 tī 虛擬 ê 未來都市--ni̍h 弄險，kap 道德兩難 kap 科技 ê 衝擊 相拄。
---
Definition: A science fiction genre often set in futuristic urban environments characterized by advanced technology, social decay, stark wealth disparity, and themes related to cybernetics, artificial intelligence, and human augmentation. Cyberpunk works often explore the impact of technology on human society, identity, and morality, frequently featuring dystopian settings.

Ex 1: The film "Blade Runner" is a classic example of the cyberpunk genre. Set in a futuristic Los Angeles with advanced technology but plagued by environmental pollution, social inequality, and moral decay, the film explores themes of artificial intelligence, human identity, and the consequences of technological advancement. The film's exploration of replicants raises questions about the nature of humanity, the possibility of AI sentience, and the blurring lines between humans and machines. The film's visual style, with its dark cityscape, flickering neon lights, and rain-soaked atmosphere, further contributes to its iconic cyberpunk aesthetic.

Ex 2: The Japanese anime film "Akira" is another iconic cyberpunk work. Set in a rebuilt Tokyo with advanced technology but still grappling with social unrest, the film delves into themes of youth rebellion, gang violence, and government conspiracies. Its visual style, featuring neon lights, towering skyscrapers, and a sense of speed, is highly memorable. "Akira" explores the concept of psychic powers, prompting us to consider the limits of human potential and the unforeseen consequences of unchecked technological advancement. The film's depiction of Tokyo's destruction and reconstruction symbolizes the interplay between technological progress and societal upheaval, showcasing a quintessential cyberpunk worldview.

Ex 3: Cyberpunk has also influenced fashion, music, and game design. Cyberpunk fashion often incorporates leather, metal, and fluorescent colors to convey a futuristic and rebellious aesthetic. Cyberpunk music frequently utilizes electronic music, synthesizers, and industrial noises to create a dark and mysterious atmosphere. Cyberpunk games typically allow players to explore virtual futuristic cities, confronting moral dilemmas and the impact of technology.

7. Á-té-kho͘h、妝娗藝術 (POJ: Á-té-kho͘h、Chng-thāⁿ gē-su̍t, 英: Art Deco, 日: アール・デコ)

8. Pá-lok (POJ: Pá-lok, 英: Baroque, 日: バロック)

定義：一款 tī 16 世紀尾到 18 世紀中 tī 歐美 chhiaⁿ-iāⁿ(奢颺) ê 藝術 kap 建築風格，特徵是強調動感、戲劇性、kap 感情 ê 表現，捷捷用彎斡 ê 曲線、複雜 ê 構圖、kap 誇張 ê 妝娗，來 hō͘ 人感覺雄偉、華麗、koh 有宗教性 ê 氛圍。

例 1：羅馬 ê 聖 Pí-tek(Peter) 大教堂，是巴洛克建築 ê 代表作之一。伊 ê 外觀，有濟濟雕像、圓柱、kap 拱門，重重疊疊，hō͘ 人感覺雄偉 koh 壯觀。教堂內底，天花板頂懸 ê 壁畫，描畫著聖經故事，色水鮮沢，人物表情生動，hō͘ 人感覺身歷其境。koh-khah 要緊--ê 是，教堂中央 ê 圓頂，hō͘ 人感覺空間無限延伸，光線 ùi 圓頂 ê 窗仔 iā(掖)--落來，kā 規間教堂照 kah 光爍爍，hō͘ 人感覺神聖 koh 莊嚴。巴洛克建築 ê 戲劇性 kap 宗教性，tī 遮个空間--ni̍h，表現 kah 足透澈。

例 2：義大利畫家 Kā-la̍h-oa-chih(Caravaggio) ê 作品，是巴洛克繪畫 ê 代表。伊 ê 畫，捷捷用 chiah-kng(劁光) ê 手法，kā 人物 ùi 烏暗 ê 背景--ni̍h iáⁿ-ba̍k(影目)--出來，hō͘ 人感覺戲劇性 koh 真實。伊 ê 人物表情，定定足誇張，koh 飽滇感情，hō͘ 觀者 ē-tàng 感受著畫--nih ê 故事 kap 情緒。譬論講，伊 ê 名畫《聖 Má-thài(Mathew) 受召》，描畫著Iâ-so͘(Jesus) 招聖 Má-thài 做門徒 ê 時陣，光線 ùi 窗仔 iā--入來，直接 iā tī 耶穌 kap 聖馬太 ê 身軀頂懸，其他人物煞 tī 烏暗--ni̍h，án-ne ê 對比，hō͘ 觀者 ê 注意力集中 tī 遮个重要人物，koh 加強著戲劇性 ê 效果。

Definition: An artistic and architectural style that flourished in Europe and America from the late 16th to mid-18th centuries, characterized by its emphasis on dynamism, theatricality, and emotional expression, often employing dramatic curves, complex compositions, and elaborate ornamentation to create a sense of grandeur, opulence, and religious fervor.

Ex 1: St. Peter's Basilica in Rome is a prime example of Baroque architecture. Its facade, adorned with numerous statues, columns, and arches, creates a sense of overwhelming grandeur and monumentality. Inside the basilica, the ceiling frescoes depict biblical scenes with vibrant colors and expressive figures, immersing viewers in the narrative. Moreover, the central dome creates a feeling of infinite space, with light pouring in through its windows, illuminating the entire basilica and evoking a sense of divinity and solemnity. The theatricality and religious fervor of Baroque architecture are fully realized in this awe-inspiring space.

Ex 2: The works of Italian painter Caravaggio are iconic examples of Baroque painting. His paintings often utilize dramatic chiaroscuro, highlighting figures against dark backgrounds to create a sense of theatricality and realism. His figures often display exaggerated expressions and intense emotions, allowing viewers to connect with the stories and sentiments depicted in the paintings. For instance, his masterpiece "The Calling of St. Matthew" portrays the moment when Jesus calls Matthew to be his disciple. Light streams in from a window, directly illuminating Jesus and Matthew while leaving other figures in shadow. This contrast focuses the viewer's attention on these key figures and enhances the dramatic impact of the scene.
